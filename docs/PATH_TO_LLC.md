### 1.3 Trademark filing prognosis

High probability of successful registration in Class 9 (downloadable educational game software) and Class 41 (educational services) via TEAS Plus intent-to-use filing. Two specific risks to manage at filing time:

1. **Opposition risk during USPTO publication period** (the 30-day window when third parties can object). ## Step 2 — Publisher Name Selection — ✅ COMPLETED (April 9, 2026)

### 2.1 Levvl reassessment — ❌ FAILED

Levvl was originally selected as the publisher name on the basis of a USPTO TESS exact-match search returning zero hits. The original document treated common-law clearance as a five-minute formality. A proper second-pass run on April 9, 2026 turned up disqualifying evidence:

**What was checked:** Google searches for `"Levvl"`, `"Levvl" app`, `"Levvl" software`, `"Levvl" education`.

**What was found:**

1. **Levvl Limited (UK)** — `levvl.co.uk` — UK holding company for "privacy-first digital products." Adjacent industry, exact spelling match.

2. **Levvel** (with the extra "e") — Dutch consulting firm with multiple US App Store apps including Levvel Fitness, Lev by Levvel, and Levvel App for health. One letter off, phonetically identical.

3. **CRITICAL FIND: Levvel LLC** (Charlotte, NC) — Software consulting firm founded 2013, ~172 employees. **Acquired by Endava (NYSE: DAVA) in April 2021.** Endava owns the registered USPTO trademark **LEVVELUP** (serial 87007575, REGISTERED) in Class 41 — Educational services for software conferences. This is the same class Mapolis would file in. The combination of (a) phonetic near-identity ("Levvl" / "Levvel"), (b) related goods (educational software), and (c) ownership by a deep-pocketed publicly traded company creates meaningful opposition risk during USPTO publication.

**Decision:** Levvl is abandoned as the publisher name. The original USPTO-only search was insufficient because it didn't catch the registered LEVVELUP mark (which contains the phonetic root) or the substantial common-law commercial use of "Levvel" by the Endava-owned entity. Going forward, every publisher candidate must clear both USPTO **and** a thorough Google common-law pass before being committed to.

## Step 3 — Form Gnalopey LLC

Once both names are cleared (Steps 1 and 2 — both complete), form the legal entity. The LLC is what will own the domain, the trademark application, the Apple Developer account ($99/year), the Google Play Developer account ($25 one-time), the Stripe account, the privacy policy, and any future revenue.

### 3.1 State selection

Form in your home state by default. Common alternatives:
- **Delaware** — popular for startups expecting venture funding; not necessary for a solo project; adds annual fees and a registered agent
- **Wyoming** — cheapest annual fees, strong privacy, popular for solo operators; requires a registered agent if you don't live there
- **Your home state (Maryland)** — simplest, you're the registered agent, no out-of-state filings; recommended unless you have a specific reason not to

For a kids' education app run by a sole proprietor with no investor plans, Maryland is the right choice.

### 3.2 Name availability check (state level) — ✅ CONFIRMED AVAILABLE (April 9, 2026)

The Maryland SDAT Business Entity Search at `egov.maryland.gov/businessexpress/entitysearch` returned **zero matches** for the partial-name query "mapolis". No active, forfeited, dissolved, or cancelled Maryland entity uses any variation of the Mapolis stem. **Run the same search for "Gnalopey" before filing Gnalopey LLC.**

*Optional but worth considering:* file a Name Reservation Application with SDAT for $25 to lock the name for 30 days if there will be any gap between today and submission of Articles of Organization.

### 3.3 Filing

You have three options:
- **DIY through Maryland Business Express** — cheapest (~$100 filing fee), takes 30 minutes
- **LegalZoom or Northwest Registered Agent** — $200-400, handles paperwork, includes registered agent
- **Local business attorney** — $500-1500, full setup including operating agreement and EIN

For a beta-stage solo project, DIY or LegalZoom is fine. A lawyer is worth it if you anticipate complex ownership, hiring employees, or taking outside money.

### 3.4 Post-formation tasks

- [ ] **Get an EIN** from IRS.gov (free, immediate, online) — needed for bank accounts, Stripe, Apple Developer
- [ ] **Open a business bank account** in the LLC's name — Mercury, Relay, or your local bank
- [ ] **Draft an operating agreement** even if you're the sole member — Maryland doesn't strictly require one but it's standard practice; LegalZoom includes a template
- [ ] **Register for any required state/local business licenses** — varies by Maryland county and city

## Step 5 — File Trademark Applications (Optional but Recommended)

Not legally required to operate, but cheap insurance for a brand you're investing real time in. Can be filed on an "intent to use" basis before launch.

### 5.1 What to file

Two applications:

- **Application 1: "Mapolis Games"** — Class 9 (downloadable educational game software) and Class 41 (educational services). The publisher mark. Treat "Games" as descriptive; the protected element is "Mapolis Games" as a unit, with "Mapolis" carrying the distinctive weight.
- **Application 2: "Mapolis"** — Class 9 and Class 41. The product mark, separate from the publisher mark, so it survives even if the publisher entity ever changes.

Filing both gives the publisher and product independent protection. Total cost at TEAS Plus rates: 4 class filings × $250 = **$1,000** plus the attorney consultation if you take it.

### 5.2 Class strategy — Skip Class 28

**Do NOT file in Class 28 (toys and games)** even though it might seem natural for a game publisher. Class 28 is where North Star Games (Reg. 4905664) operates and where they killed the original "North Star Global" application. Avoiding Class 28 means the new Mapolis applications don't run into the same examiner who rejected the previous filing, and it sidesteps any defensive watch-list flags that mark may carry. Class 28 can be added later if/when physical merchandise becomes a real product line.

### 5.3 How to file

- **DIY through TEAS Plus** at uspto.gov — cheapest at $250 per class per mark, suitable if you have clean clearance and can describe your goods/services from the standard ID Manual
- **LegalZoom** — $400-700 per mark, handles the form
- **Trademark attorney** — $500-1500 per mark, recommended if any clearance results were ambiguous, or if you want a professional opinion on strength

For "Mapolis" and "Mapolis Games", an attorney consultation is worth the money (~$300–500) because of the German mapolis AG common-law presence on the product mark. They have no US registration and operate in a different category, so opposition is unlikely to succeed — but they're the kind of mid-sized European company that *could* file a notice during the USPTO publication period, and getting a professional opinion on application strength before filing is cheap insurance against having to defend an opposition.

## Reference: Why This Order Matters

A common mistake is to register the domain first (it's $12, feels concrete, gives you something to point at) and then later discover the name is trademarked or the LLC name is taken. The right order is:

1. **Trademark first** — free, fastest to fail. A name that fails trademark can't be saved by anything downstream.
2. **LLC second** — names the legal entity that will own everything else.
3. **Domain third** — registered in the LLC's name so it's a business asset, not a personal one.
4. **Trademark filing last** — files the formal claim once you know the name is yours.

Doing it in any other order means you'll throw work away if anything fails.

**A second lesson learned the hard way (April 9, 2026):** USPTO TESS clearance alone is NOT sufficient. Every name candidate must clear BOTH a USPTO search AND a thorough Google common-law pass before being committed to. The original Levvl selection skipped the second step and missed the LEVVELUP / Endava finding entirely. Don't repeat this mistake.
